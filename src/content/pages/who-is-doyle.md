## Who is Sir Arthur Conan Doyle?

Sir Arthur Ignatius Conan Doyle (1859–1930) was a Scottish writer best known for creating Sherlock Holmes, literature's most famous detective. Born in Edinburgh to Roman Catholic parents, Doyle studied medicine at Edinburgh University and qualified as a doctor in 1885, practicing as an eye specialist before becoming a full-time writer in 1891.

### Literary Career

Doyle's first Holmes story, *A Study in Scarlet*, appeared in 1887 and introduced Dr. Watson and the iconic 221B Baker Street address. Though he grew weary of the detective by 1893, killing Holmes off in "The Final Problem," public demand led him to resurrect the character in 1903's "The Empty House." Holmes stories appeared in five collections spanning 1892–1927.

### Other Works & Interests

Beyond Holmes, Doyle wrote historical novels, plays, and the *Professor Challenger* series, including *The Lost World*. He served as a physician during the Boer War and was knighted in 1902. His later life centered on spiritualism—a pursuit sparked by his son Kingsley's death in World War I. He spent over a million dollars investigating fairies and the supernatural.

### Legacy

By 1920, Doyle ranked among the world's highest-paid writers. Holmes stories have been translated into 50+ languages and adapted extensively for stage, film, and television, cementing Doyle's enduring cultural impact.
